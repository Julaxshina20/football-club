About Our Club

Discover the legacy and passion of HALA MADRID J&J

Our Story

Founded in 1902, JOE FOOTBALL CLUB has grown from a local football club to one of the most prestigious and successful football institutions in the world. With a rich history spanning over a century, our club has become synonymous with excellence, passion, and a commitment to beautiful football.

Our journey has been marked by legendary players, iconic matches, and an unwavering support from our fans, known as the 'Madridistas'. We have always strived to uphold the values of respect, teamwork, and the pursuit of victory.

Today, JOE FOOTBALL CLUB continues to build on its legacy, investing in youth development, state-of-the-art facilities, and a global outreach program to spread the love of football.

Our History

A journey through time - from humble beginnings to global giants

Club Timeline

Key moments in our rich history

1902 - Foundation

JOE FOOTBALL CLUB was founded by a group of football enthusiasts in Madrid. The club's original colors were blue and white.

1920 - First Championship

The club won its first major trophy, the Copa del Rey, marking the beginning of a successful era.

1947 - New Stadium

The iconic Santiago Bernabéu Stadium was inaugurated with a capacity of 75,000 spectators.

1956 - European Glory

Won the first of many European Cups, establishing the club as a continental powerhouse.

1985 - Youth Academy

La Fábrica, the club's renowned youth academy, was established to develop future stars.

2000 - Galácticos Era

The club entered a new era of global superstars, signing world-class players like Zidane, Ronaldo, and Beckham.

2014 - La Décima

After a 12-year wait, the club won its 10th European Cup, cementing its status as the most successful club in the competition.

2023 - Modern Era

The club completes a €500 million renovation of the Santiago Bernabéu, creating the most advanced stadium in the world.

Our Home: Santiago Bernabéu

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is more than just a football ground - it's a symbol of our club's history and ambition. Since its inauguration in 1947, it has witnessed countless historic moments and hosted some of football's greatest matches.

With a current capacity of 81,044, the Bernabéu is one of the world's most iconic sporting venues. The recent renovation has transformed it into a state-of-the-art facility with a retractable roof, expanded hospitality areas, and a 360-degree video screen.

For our fans, visiting the Bernabéu is a pilgrimage, an experience that captures the essence of Madridismo and the passion that defines our club.
